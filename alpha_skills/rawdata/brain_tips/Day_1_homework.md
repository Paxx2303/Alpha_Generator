### Comment by TP14664 1 year ago
Neutralization, as you’ve mentioned, is a strategy designed to **reduce market exposure** and minimize **systematic risk** by allocating an equal amount of capital to both long (buying) and short (selling) positions. This strategy aims to neutralize the impact of **market-wide movements** (i.e., market going up or down) and focuses the performance on the **relative performance** of the securities involved rather than overall market movements.

### Comment by DP11917 1 year ago
Neutralization, in the context of quantitative finance, is a strategy aimed at reducing exposure to systematic risk by balancing long and short positions in such a way that the overall portfolio remains "market-neutral." By allotting equal amounts of capital to long and short positions, you effectively neutralize the risk that arises from broader market movements (such as general market upswings or downswings).
