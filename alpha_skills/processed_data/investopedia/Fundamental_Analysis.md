# Fundamental Analysis

**Source:** Investopedia / Quantitative Finance Reference

Fundamental analysis is a method used to evaluate a security or asset by attempting to measure its **intrinsic value**. It involves examining related economic, financial, and other qualitative and quantitative factors to determine whether an asset is overvalued or undervalued by the market.

### Key Components of Fundamental Analysis
The process typically involves studying:

* **Macroeconomic Factors:** Analysts look at the "big picture," including the overall economy, industry conditions, interest rates, inflation, and GDP.
* **Company-Specific (Microeconomic) Factors:** This includes evaluating a company's individual financial health, such as:
 * **Financial Statements:** Reviewing income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements.
 * **Financial Metrics:** Using ratios like the price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio to gauge value.
 * **Qualitative Factors:** Assessing management quality, business model, and competitive position within the industry.

### The Goal
The primary objective of fundamental analysis is to identify a value for an asset that an investor can compare against the current market price. 
* **Undervalued:** If the intrinsic value is higher than the current market price, the asset may be considered a good buy.
* **Overvalued:** If the intrinsic value is lower than the current market price, it may be a signal to sell.

Fundamental analysis is often contrasted with **technical analysis**, which focuses on market sentiment, price patterns, and historical trading data rather than the underlying business or economic factors.
