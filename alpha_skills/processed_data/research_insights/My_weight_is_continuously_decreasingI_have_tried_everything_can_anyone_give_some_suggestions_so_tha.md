### Comment by TT55495 1 year ago
It sounds like you're on the right track by trying to diversify your alphas and targeting high-representation regions. However, to see an increase in your weight factor, you might want to focus on improving the **quality** and **robustness** of your alphas. Here are a few additional suggestions:

1. **Evaluate Alpha Performance Across Different Market Conditions**: Test your alphas in various market regimes (bull, bear, and sideways markets) to ensure they’re adaptable and not just optimized for specific conditions. This can help improve their overall performance consistency.
2. **Refine Signal-to-Noise Ratio**: Ensure your alphas are focused on high-value signals and not just capturing market noise. Try reducing unnecessary features or noise-prone data points and ensure that your alphas are statistically significant.
3. **Incorporate Risk-Adjusted Metrics**: Rather than focusing solely on returns, improve the risk-adjusted performance of your alphas (e.g., maximizing Sharpe ratio). Use risk management techniques such as volatility control or dynamic position sizing to boost the reliability and stability of your signals.
4. **Explore Less Competitive Markets**: In addition to the commonly represented regions like USA or EUR, consider exploring emerging markets or asset classes with lower competition. These areas may offer hidden opportunities that have been overlooked by more generalized strategies.
5. **Focus on Long-Term Alpha Stability**: Ensure your alphas are not just overfitting to short-term trends or recent market movements. A long-term, stable alpha will likely improve your weight factor over time as it demonstrates reliable, consistent performance.

By continuously evaluating and refining these aspects, you'll likely see improvements in your weight factor and overall performance.
