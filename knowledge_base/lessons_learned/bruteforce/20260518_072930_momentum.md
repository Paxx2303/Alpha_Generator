### 2. Structural Pattern Behind the Winners  
Winning expressions share the form:  

```
rank( ts_mean( returns, <lookback> ) )
```

- **`returns`** captures the raw price momentum.  
- **`ts_mean(..., <lookback>)`** smooths the return series, filtering out day├бРѓгРђўto├бРѓгРђўday noise while preserving the trend over the chosen horizon.  
- **`rank(...)`** converts the smoothed signal into a cross├бРѓгРђўsectional ranking, making the alpha neutral to overall market level and reducing the impact of outliers.  

The look├бРѓгРђўback of **5├бРѓгРђў20 days** balances two opposing needs:  
- Short enough to react to fresh price movements (capturing the momentum factor).  
- Long enough to average out random fluctuations, thereby improving the signal├бРѓгРђўto├бРѓгРђўnoise ratio and lowering turnover.

## 2. What Structural Pattern Makes the Winners Good

The single winning expression is:

```
rank(volume / ts_mean(volume, 3))
```

with settings: `neutralization=SUBINDUSTRY`, `truncation=0.05`, `decay=2`, `delay=1`.

**Why this works:**

- **Ratio form (normalized volume):** `volume / ts_mean(volume, 3)` is a relative measure Рђћ it asks "is today's volume unusual compared to the recent 3-day baseline?" This is inherently more robust than a raw level because it auto-scales across large-cap and small-cap names.
- **Rank operator:** Applying `rank()` cross-sectionally converts the raw ratio into a uniform score, removing distributional assumptions and making the signal portfolio-constructible.
- **SUBINDUSTRY neutralization:** This is the critical differentiator. The same expression with `neutralization=INDUSTRY` produces Sharpe 0.0 and fitness 0.0. SUBINDUSTRY neutralization removes finer-grained sector bets, which means the alpha is capturing something *within* sub-industries Рђћ likely the information content of volume spikes that is independent of sector rotation. This is a well-known pattern: volume-based signals often carry sector-confused momentum, and tighter neutralization purifies them.
- **Short lookback (3 days):** For volume confirmation, a short window captures the immediacy of volume spikes. Volume is a flow variable Рђћ it decays in information value much faster than price. A 3-day window is appropriate; a 20-day window would dilute the signal with stale data.
- **Decay=2:** The moderate decay smooths the signal slightly without killing the responsiveness that makes volume signals valuable.

**The structural winning pattern is: normalized flow variable + cross-sectional rank + tight neutralization.**

## 3. Why the Losers Fail

### `price_vs_mean` Рђћ structurally degenerate

```

```

This expression computes the 3-day return (close relative to its 3-day mean) and ranks it. It fails because:

- **Lookback=3 is too short for price momentum.** Price series are much noisier than volume series at short horizons. A 3-day price mean is dominated by the current price itself, so `close / ts_mean(close, 3) - 1` is approximately `close / ((close + close[1] + close[2])/3) - 1`, which is a very noisy, mean-reverting quantity rather than a trend indicator.
- **No approval, zero Sharpe, zero fitness, zero turnover:** The signal is so flat that it generates no trades. This suggests the expression, after neutralization and truncation, is reduced to near-zero across the universe. Price-vs-mean at 3 days is essentially pure noise once you strip out industry effects.

**Root causes:**
