## Hermes Analysis

**Analysis of Brute-Force WorldQuant Alpha Results: Momentum Strategy**

---

### 1. **Strongest Expression Family**

The only family present in this brute-force sweep is **`return_persistence`**, which encapsulates momentum-like signals based on ranked time-series means of returns over varying lookback windows (3, 5, 10, 20, 40, 60 days). Despite being the sole family, it demonstrates clear internal differentiation:

- **Best performer**: `rank(ts_mean(returns, 5))`  
  - Sharpe = **2.448**, Fitness = **1.926**
- **Family average**: Sharpe = **1.403**, Fitness = **1.714**

Thus, **`return_persistence` is not only the strongest but the only viable family** in this batch. However, its efficacy is highly sensitive to the lookback window.

> 🔍 **Insight**: The family’s strength lies in capturing short-to-intermediate-term price continuation—consistent with classic momentum theory (Jegadeesh & Titman, 1993). But performance degrades sharply beyond ~20 days, suggesting mean-reversion or noise dominates at longer horizons.

---

### 2. **Structural Pattern of Winning Expressions**

All top candidates share a **minimalist, normalized momentum structure**:

- **Core operator**: `rank(...)` → ensures cross-sectional comparability and robustness to outliers.
- **Signal**: `ts_mean(returns, N)` → simple rolling average of daily returns (i.e., cumulative return over N days, scaled).
- **Optimal lookback**: **5 days** wins decisively.

**Why this works**:
- **Short lookback (5d)** captures fresh momentum before it decays or reverses.
- `rank()` neutralizes sector/market-wide moves (implicit sector neutrality).
- Low complexity avoids overfitting—critical given the failure lessons from overly noisy or unnormalized signals (e.g., `ts_delta(close,10)/ts_std_dev(close,10)` failed due to lack of normalization and high self-correlation).

> ✅ **Winning formula**: *“Rank of recent return persistence”* = clean, fast, decorrelated momentum.

---

### 3. **Why the Losers Fail**

Bottom performers include longer lookbacks (`ts_mean(returns, 60)`) and suboptimal short ones (`ts_mean(returns, 3)`):

| Expression | Issue |
|-----------|-------|
| `ts_mean(returns, 60)` | **Over-smoothed signal**: 60-day average dilutes recent momentum; high autocorrelation → stale positions → poor turnover-adjusted returns (turnover = 0.149, lowest in set). Also vulnerable to regime shifts. |
| `ts_mean(returns, 3)` | **Too noisy**: 3-day mean is hypersensitive to microstructure noise, gaps, and reversals. Low returns (0.058) despite moderate Sharpe—fails to generate alpha after costs. |
| `ts_mean(returns, 10)` | **Ambiguous zone**: Not short enough to be fresh, not long enough to be stable. Mid-tier Sharpe (0.938) but high fitness (2.152)—likely overfits in-sample. |

> ❌ **Failure modes**:  
> - **Long lookbacks** → low turnover, high self-correlation, sluggish response.  
> - **Very short lookbacks** → noise-dominated, poor risk-adjusted returns.  
> Both violate the “Goldilocks zone” of momentum: **5–10 days** appears optimal here.

---

### 4. **Recommended Brute-Force Expansions**

Based on structural insights and failure recovery lessons, prioritize these **next-step expansions**:

#### A. **Hybrid Momentum + Orthogonal Signals**
Leverage the winning `rank(ts_mean(returns,5))` as a base and **add decorrelated components**:
- `rank(ts_mean(returns,5)) + α * ts_corr(close, volume, 20)`  
  → Adds volume-price divergence signal (orthogonal to pure return momentum).
- `rank(ts_mean(returns,5)) - β * ts_delta(returns,1)`  
  → Introduces short-term mean-reversion to reduce drawdowns (per failure recovery lesson).

#### B. **Adaptive Lookback Windows**
Test **dynamic lookbacks** based on volatility regimes:
- `rank(ts_mean(returns, N))` where `N = clip(volatility_rank * 10, 3, 20)`  
  → Shorter windows in high-vol (fast momentum), longer in low-vol (stable trends).

#### C. **Normalized Momentum Variants**
Replace raw `ts_mean(returns,N)` with **risk-adjusted versions**:
- `rank(ts_mean(returns,5) / ts_std(returns,5))` → Sharpe-like momentum.
- `rank(ts_mean(returns,5) / ts_max_drawdown(returns,5))` → Drawdown-aware momentum.

#### D. **Sector-Neutralized Momentum**
Explicitly neutralize within sectors:
- `rank(industry_neutralize(ts_mean(returns,5)))`  
  → Reduces sector concentration risk (aligned with WQ’s neutralization practices).

> 🧪 **Brute-force priority**: Start with **(A)**—it directly applies proven failure-recovery tactics and builds on the strongest base signal.

---

### Final Note

While `return_persistence` dominates this sweep, **true alpha durability requires blending**. The best standalone momentum signal (`rank(ts_mean(returns,5))`) should be treated as a **core building block**, not a final product. Future brute-force runs should explore **multi-signal composites** that maintain the winner’s speed while adding robustness through orthogonal diversification and adaptive risk control.
