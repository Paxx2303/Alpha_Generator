We present explicit formulas – that are also computer code – for 
101 real-life quantitative trading alphas.  Their average holding

indirect empirical analysis.  We further find empirically that

## Applied Lessons
Looking at the failure diagnostics, here's my analysis and remediation:

**Failure Diagnosis:**

1. **Self-correlation 0.86 > 0.70** â€” This is the most critical issue. The 60-day `ts_std_dev` and 5-day `ts_corr` windows create heavy overlap with the 10-day `ts_mean`, causing the signal to be too sticky/persistent. High self-correlation means the alpha isn't rotating fast enough â€” it's essentially the same signal day-over-day.

2. **Drawdown 34% > 30%** â€” The momentum component (`ts_mean(returns,10)/ts_std_dev(returns,60)`) is a classic momentum-over-volatility ratio that suffers in regime changes. When momentum reversals happen, this ratio whipsaws badly.

3. **Sharpe 0.91 < 1.50, Fitness 0.57 < 1.00** â€” Downstream consequences of the above two issues. The signal is too smooth (high self-correlation) and has tail risk (drawdown), which crushes risk-adjusted returns.

**Remediation Plan:**

- **Reduce self-correlation:** Shorten lookback windows to make the signal more responsive. Replace the 60-day std_dev with a shorter window, and reduce the correlation window. Add `ts_delta` to introduce a change-based component that decorrelates from the level.
- **Reduce drawdown:** Replace the raw momentum/volatility ratio with a `zscore`-normalized version, which adapts to current regime. Use `vwap` instead of `close` for the correlation component to get a cleaner price-volume relationship.
- **Improve fitness/Sharpe:** Add a mean-reversion overlay via `ts_delta` on volume to capture short-term flow signals that are orthogonal to the momentum base.

**Key adjustments:**
- `ts_std_dev(returns, 60)` â†’ `ts_std_dev(returns, 20)` â€” shorter vol window, less persistence
- `ts_corr(volume, returns, 5)` â†’ `ts_corr(volume, ts_delta(returns, 3), 10)` â€” correlate volume with returns *changes* (flow vs. momentum), slightly longer window for stability
- Wrap momentum in `zscore` for regime adaptation
- Use `ts_delta(volume, 1)` as a secondary signal for turnover
