The original expression: `rank(ts_mean(returns, 5) / ts_std_dev(returns, 20)) * rank(1 - ts_corr(volume, returns, 60))`

**Diagnosis:**
- The 5-day mean return / 20-day std dev is a short-term Sharpe-like signal â€” too noisy, prone to reversals and poor sub-universe performance
- `1 - ts_corr(volume, returns, 60)` is a long-lookback contrarian signal â€” when volume and returns are positively correlated, this goes negative, which may be counter-trend in ways that hurt in certain regimes
- Multiplying two rank signals creates high concentration in tails â†’ high turnover
- No `zscore` normalization means the raw rank product can be unstable across sub-universes

**Remediation adjustments:**

1. **Operator**: Replace the raw ratio `ts_mean/std_dev` with separate `rank(ts_mean(...))` and `rank(ts_std_dev(...))` components, then combine additively rather than multiplicatively to reduce tail concentration. Use `zscore` on the final combined signal to normalize across sub-universes.

2. **Turnover**: Shorten the longest lookback (60â†’20) to reduce signal lag-induced turnover. Add `ts_delta` for a smoother trend component instead of relying solely on `ts_mean` which flips sign rapidly.

3. **Correlation (self-correlation)**: Change the structure enough to differentiate from the original â€” use `vwap` instead of `close`-derived returns in one leg, and use `ts_delta` of volume rather than raw volume in the correlation term.

4. **Drawdown / sub-universe Sharpe**: Use `zscore` wrapping to prevent extreme weights in small sub-universes. Replace the `1 - corr(...)` contrarian formulation with a momentum-aligned `ts_corr(ts_delta(volume,5), returns, 20)` which is more robust.
